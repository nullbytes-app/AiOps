## Roadmap Principles

### 1. Goal-Driven & Feature-Driven Balance (2025 Best Practice)

**Goal-Driven (70%):** Focus on strategic objectives (client onboarding, time reduction, revenue)
**Feature-Driven (30%):** Respond to client feedback and market demands

**Application:**
- Epic 6 (Admin UI): Goal-driven (enables multi-tenant scaling)
- RCA Agent: Feature-driven (client feedback)
- Epic 7 (Plugin Architecture): Goal-driven (market expansion)

### 2. Validated Learning & Iteration

**Principle:** Ship, measure, learn, iterate

**Application:**
- Epic 6 â†’ Measure: Tenant onboarding time reduction
- RCA Agent â†’ Measure: Technician rating, time savings
- Epic 7 â†’ Measure: Multi-tool adoption rate, sales pipeline

### 3. Technical Debt Budget (20-30% per Sprint)

**Principle:** Maintain strong technical foundation (2025 best practice: 20-30% debt allocation)

**Application:**
- 10 high-priority debt items (from Technical Debt Register) â†’ 5 sprints (10 weeks)
- Parallel to epic development (not separate debt sprints)

### 4. Team Velocity-Based Planning

**Baseline Velocity:** 42 stories in 4 weeks = 10.5 stories/week (MVP delivery)

**Adjusted Velocity (Conservative):**
- Account for:
  - Onboarding time (new team members)
  - Context switching (support, bug fixes)
  - Technical debt paydown (20% allocation)
- **Adjusted:** 7-8 stories/week (~35 stories/month)

**Validation:** Re-assess after Epic 6 (Q4 2025)

### 5. Treat Roadmap as Living Document

**Principle:** Update roadmap quarterly based on retrospectives and market feedback

**Review Cadence:**
- **Monthly:** Progress check, risk assessment
- **Quarterly:** Retrospective, roadmap adjustment, reprioritization
- **Annually:** Strategic planning, multi-year vision
